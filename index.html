I am a PhD candidate in the College of Economics and Finance at Hanyang University.

I am an applied microeconomist with research interests spanning public and labor economics. My work uses Korean microdata and causal inference methods to study how taxation, social policy, and life-course shocks shape the health, labor supply, and well-being of households.

My fields are public economics, taxation, labor economics, health economics, and family economics.
